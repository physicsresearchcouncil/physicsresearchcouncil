# Sponsor Outreach & Proposal Templates
**Physics Research Council**  
*Contact: physicsresearchcouncil@gmail.com*

Operating on a zero-budget model means we rely on strategic partnerships to provide prizes, software licenses, or learning resources for our top competitors. This document contains professional outreach templates to send to potential sponsors (e.g., scientific book publishers, online course platforms, coding bootcamps, and educational software companies).

---

## 1. Zero-Budget Sponsorship Strategy
When approaching sponsors with no budget, do not ask for cash. Instead, ask for **in-kind sponsorships** that cost the company very little but hold high value for students.

**Examples of In-Kind Sponsorships to Request:**
*   **Book Publishers:** Donation of physical physics textbooks, e-books, or discount vouchers.
*   **EdTech Platforms:** Free licenses or subscription passes (e.g., 3-month premium access to Brilliant.org, Wolfram Alpha, or Coursera).
*   **Scientific Hardware Companies:** Low-cost experimental kits or sensor probes.
*   **STEM Organizations:** Free membership keys, scientific magazine subscriptions, or waiver codes.

**What We Offer Sponsors in Return:**
*   **Logo Placement:** Prominent placement on our website repository and email announcements.
*   **Certificate Branding:** The sponsor's logo featured on the official digital certificates sent to all participants, medallists, and volunteers.
*   **Round Naming Rights:** e.g., "The [Sponsor Name] Conceptual Quiz" or "The [Sponsor Name] Research Award."
*   **Direct Talent Pipeline:** Direct visibility among high-achieving high school and college STEM students.
