# Advisor & Professional Member Invitation Templates
**Physics Research Council**  
*Contact: physicsresearchcouncil@gmail.com*

To ensure the scientific credibility of our competition and resources, we invite university professors, postdoctoral researchers, graduate students, and high school educators to join our **Advisory Board** as honorary members. This document contains formal, professional invitation templates.

---

## 1. Value Proposition for Advisors
Since we cannot offer financial compensation, we emphasize the professional and social value of joining:
*   **Minimal Time Commitment:** Under 2–4 hours per semester. Tasks are asynchronous, such as reviewing 2-3 student portfolios or reviewing a physics question set.
*   **Public Recognition:** Listing on our landing page and public repository as a Scientific Board Member.
*   **Official Credentials:** An annual digital **Certificate of Appointment to the Advisory Board** of the Physics Research Council.
*   **Mentorship and Recruiting:** Opportunities to identify and mentor exceptional STEM students who may apply to their university departments.

---

## 2. Cold Email Template to University Professors / Researchers
**Subject:** Invitation: Joining the Advisory Board of the Physics Research Council (STEM Outreach)

**Dear Professor [Last Name],**

My name is [Your Name], and I am writing to you on behalf of the **Physics Research Council** (physicsresearchcouncil@gmail.com), a student-led global initiative dedicated to expanding open access to physics competitions and research tools for secondary and college students.

We are preparing to launch our annual international competition, **The Physics Championship**. This event is designed to challenge students across three divisions through conceptual tests, research portfolio showcases, and rigorous olympiad-level physics problem-solving.

To maintain the highest levels of academic and scientific rigor, we are establishing our **Scientific Advisory Board**. Given your outstanding work in [mention professor's research field, e.g., Quantum Dynamics / Experimental Astrophysics / Condensed Matter Physics] at [University/Institution Name], we would be deeply honored to invite you to join our board as an honorary academic member.

**What is expected of our Advisors?**
Advisory Board membership is voluntary, remote, and designed to respect your busy academic schedule. Responsibilities are fully asynchronous and require minimal time (approximately 2–4 hours over the entire semester):
*   **Validation:** Reviewing or providing feedback on our proposed Olympiad problem sets (Round 3) to ensure scientific accuracy.
*   **Showcase Evaluation:** Serving as a guest reviewer for Round 2 student portfolios (which consist of research papers, computational physics scripts, or projects submitted by top participants).
*   **Guidance:** Providing occasional high-level feedback on the competition syllabus.

**Benefits of Joining:**
*   You will receive an official **Certificate of Advisory Board Appointment** recognizing your contributions to international youth STEM education.
*   Your name, title, and institutional affiliation will be featured prominently on our portal contributor page.
*   The opportunity to support talented, self-motivated students from underprivileged or underrepresented backgrounds.

If you are interested in joining our board or if you have any questions, please let us know by replying to this email. You can also view our current framework and documentation at our repository landing page: https://github.com/physicsresearchcouncil/physicsresearchcouncil.

Thank you very much for your time, your dedication to scientific education, and your consideration of this invitation.

Sincerely,

**[Your Name]**  
*Academic Liaison, Physics Research Council*  
*physicsresearchcouncil@gmail.com*

## 3. Email Template to High School Physics Teachers / Educators
**Subject:** Collaboration Invitation: Physics Research Council Scientific Advisory Committee

**Dear [Teacher's Name / Head of Science Department],**

I hope this email finds you well. I am reaching out from the **Physics Research Council** (physicsresearchcouncil@gmail.com) to invite you to join our **Scientific Advisory Committee** as an honorary member.

As a science educator, you are at the forefront of inspiring the next generation of researchers. Our council aims to support your efforts by providing free, high-caliber extracurricular physics challenges—specifically our upcoming global competition, **The Physics Championship**, open to high school students (classes 9–12) and undergraduates.

We would be thrilled to have your pedagogical expertise on our committee to help shape the competition.

**As a Committee Member, you would have the opportunity to:**
*   Provide feedback on the difficulty and syllabus alignment of our Round 1 (Conceptual) and Round 3 (Olympiad) physics papers.
*   Help distribute competition guidelines to talented students at your institution.
*   Receive an official **Certificate of Science Education Advisory** from the council.
*   Be publicly recognized on our website as an academic committee member.

This role is completely voluntary, remote, and highly flexible (under 2 hours per semester). 

If you would like to join us in guiding this initiative, please reply to this email, and we will send you our introductory brief. You can also review our curriculum plans at: https://github.com/physicsresearchcouncil/physicsresearchcouncil.

Thank you for your tireless dedication to physics education and for considering this opportunity.

Warm regards,

**[Your Name]**
