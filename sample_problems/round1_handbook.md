### Section 1: Classical Mechanics (Questions 1 to 18)

#### Question 1
A stone is dropped from a height H and falls under the influence of gravity. If air resistance is negligible, what is the ratio of the distances traveled by the stone in the first, second, and third seconds of its fall?
*   (A) 1:1:1
*   (B) 1:2:3
*   (C) 1:3:5
*   (D) 1:4:9
*   **Correct Answer:** (C)
*   **Explanation:** According to Galileo's law of odd numbers, the distances traveled by a falling object in successive equal intervals of time are in the ratio of consecutive odd numbers (1:3:5:7...).

#### Question 2
A block of mass M is sliding down an inclined plane of angle theta at a constant velocity. What is the net force acting on the block?
*   (A) M * g * sin(theta)
*   (B) M * g * cos(theta)
*   (C) Zero
*   (D) M * g
*   **Correct Answer:** (C)
*   **Explanation:** Since the velocity of the block is constant, its acceleration is zero. By Newton's First Law, the net force acting on the object must be zero.

#### Question 3
An astronaut in a spacecraft orbiting the Earth experiences weightlessness. Why does this occur?
*   (A) There is no gravitational force acting on the astronaut at that altitude.
*   (B) The spacecraft is in a state of perpetual free fall toward the Earth.
*   (C) The centrifugal force is slightly larger than the gravitational force.
*   (D) The atmosphere is too thin to transmit gravitational waves.
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** Both the astronaut and the spacecraft are falling toward the Earth at the same rate due to gravity, creating a state of continuous free fall, which results in weightlessness relative to the craft.

#### Question 4
A ball of mass m collides elastically and head-on with a stationary ball of mass 3m. What fraction of the initial kinetic energy of the lighter ball is transferred to the heavier ball?
*   (A) 0.25
*   (B) 0.50
*   (C) 0.75
*   (D) 1.00
*   **Correct Answer:** (C)
*   **Explanation:** Using the formula for elastic collisions, the final velocity of the target mass is v2 = 2*m1*u1 / (m1 + m2) = 2*m*u / (4*m) = u/2. The kinetic energy transferred is K2 = 0.5*(3m)*(u/2)^2 = (3/8)*m*u^2 = 0.75 of the initial kinetic energy (0.5*m*u^2).

#### Question 5
A solid sphere and a hollow sphere of the same mass and radius are released from rest at the top of an incline. If both roll without slipping, which sphere has the greater rotational kinetic energy at the bottom?
*   (A) The solid sphere
*   (B) The hollow sphere
*   (C) They have the same rotational kinetic energy
*   (D) It depends on the length of the incline
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** The hollow sphere has a larger moment of inertia, meaning it allocates a larger fraction of its total mechanical energy into rotational kinetic energy rather than translational kinetic energy.

#### Question 6
What is the dimension of the universal gravitational constant, G?
*   (A) [M^-1 L^3 T^-2]
*   (B) [M L^2 T^-2]
*   (C) [M^-2 L^3 T^-1]
*   (D) [M^-1 L^2 T^-3]
*   **Correct Answer:** (A)
*   **Explanation:** From Newton's law of gravitation, G = F * r^2 / (m1 * m2). Substituting dimensions: [M L T^-2] * [L^2] / [M^2] = [M^-1 L^3 T^-2].

#### Question 7
A car negotiates a flat, circular curve of radius R. If the coefficient of static friction between the tires and the road is mu, what is the maximum speed the car can achieve without skidding?
*   (A) Square root of (mu * g * R)
*   (B) mu * g * R
*   (C) Square root of (g * R / mu)
*   (D) mu * R / g
*   **Correct Answer:** (A)
*   **Explanation:** The centripetal force is provided by static friction: m * v^2 / R = mu * m * g. Solving for v yields the square root of (mu * g * R).

#### Question 8
A spring of force constant k is cut into two equal halves. What is the force constant of each half?
*   (A) k/2
*   (B) k
*   (C) 2k
*   (D) 4k
*   **Correct Answer:** (C)
*   **Explanation:** The stiffness of a spring is inversely proportional to its length. Halving the length doubles the spring constant.

#### Question 9
A uniform rod of length L and mass M is pivoted at one end. What is its moment of inertia about the pivot?
*   (A) (1/12) * M * L^2
*   (B) (1/3) * M * L^2
*   (C) (1/2) * M * L^2
*   (D) (2/5) * M * L^2
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** The moment of inertia of a uniform rod about its center of mass is (1/12)*M*L^2. Applying the parallel axis theorem for a distance of L/2 gives (1/12)*M*L^2 + M*(L/2)^2 = (1/3)*M*L^2.

#### Question 10
If the distance between the Earth and the Sun were suddenly halved, how many days would there be in a year?
*   (A) 182.5 days
*   (B) 129 days
*   (C) 91 days
*   (D) 45 days
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** By Kepler's Third Law, T^2 is proportional to R^3. Thus, (T2/T1)^2 = (R2/R1)^3 = (1/2)^3 = 1/8. T2 = T1 / (2 * sqrt(2)) = 365 / 2.828 ≈ 129 days.

#### Question 11
An object is thrown vertically upwards with velocity v. When it reaches half of its maximum height, what is its velocity?
*   (A) v / 2
*   (B) v / sqrt(2)
*   (C) v * sqrt(3) / 2
*   (D) v / 4
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** From v_f^2 = v^2 - 2*g*h, the maximum height is H = v^2 / (2g). At h = H/2, v_f^2 = v^2 - 2*g*(v^2/(4g)) = v^2 - v^2/2 = v^2 / 2. Thus, v_f = v / sqrt(2).

#### Question 12
A bucket full of water is rotated in a vertical circle of radius R. What is the minimum velocity at the top of the path so that no water spills out?
*   (A) sqrt(g * R)
*   (B) g * R
*   (C) sqrt(2 * g * R)
*   (D) zero
*   **Correct Answer:** (A)
*   **Explanation:** To prevent water from falling, the centripetal acceleration at the top must be at least equal to gravity: m * v^2 / R = m * g, which yields v = sqrt(g * R).

#### Question 13
Which of the following is a non-conservative force?
*   (A) Gravitational force
*   (B) Electrostatic force
*   (C) Elastic spring force
*   (D) Viscous drag force
*   **Correct Answer:** (D)
*   **Explanation:** Viscous drag force dissipates mechanical energy as heat, meaning the work done by it depends on the path taken.

#### Question 14
A disc of mass M and radius R rotates with angular velocity omega. A second identical disc is placed gently on top of the first. What is the final angular velocity of the combined system?
*   (A) omega / 4
*   (B) omega / 2
*   (C) omega
*   (D) 2 * omega
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** By conservation of angular momentum, I1 * omega = I2 * omega_f. Since the moment of inertia doubles (I2 = 2 * I1), the final angular velocity is halved.

#### Question 15
What is the acceleration due to gravity at a depth equal to R/2 inside the Earth, where R is the radius of the Earth?
*   (A) g / 4
*   (B) g / 2
*   (C) 2g
*   (D) Zero
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** Inside the Earth, gravity varies linearly with distance from the center: g(d) = g * (1 - d/R). For d = R/2, g(R/2) = g * (1 - 0.5) = g/2.

#### Question 16
A simple pendulum has a period T on Earth. What is its period on the moon, where gravity is one-sixth of the Earth's gravity?
*   (A) T / sqrt(6)
*   (B) T * sqrt(6)
*   (C) 6T
*   (D) T / 6
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** The period is T = 2 * pi * sqrt(L / g). Since gravity is divided by 6, the period increases by a factor of the square root of 6.

#### Question 17
A uniform block of mass m is pushed against a vertical wall with a horizontal force F. If the coefficient of static friction is mu, what is the minimum force F needed to prevent the block from sliding down?
*   (A) m * g
*   (B) m * g / mu
*   (C) mu * m * g
*   (D) m * g * mu
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** The normal force is N = F. The maximum static friction force is f = mu * N = mu * F. To prevent sliding, f must equal the weight: mu * F = m * g, giving F = m * g / mu.

#### Question 18
A bullet of mass m moving with speed v strikes and embeds in a block of mass M suspended by a string of length L. What is the velocity of the combined mass immediately after the collision?
*   (A) v
*   (B) m * v / (m + M)
*   (C) M * v / (m + M)
*   (D) m * v / M
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** By conservation of linear momentum, m * v = (m + M) * v_f. Solving for v_f yields m * v / (m + M).

### Section 2: Electromagnetism (Questions 19 to 32)

#### Question 19
Two point charges, +q and -q, are separated by a distance d. What is the electric potential at the midpoint between them?
*   (A) q / (2 * pi * epsilon_0 * d)
*   (B) q / (4 * pi * epsilon_0 * d)
*   (C) Zero
*   (D) 2q / (pi * epsilon_0 * d)
*   **Correct Answer:** (C)
*   **Explanation:** The potential at the midpoint is the sum of the potentials from both charges: V = V1 + V2 = q / (4 * pi * epsilon_0 * (d/2)) + (-q) / (4 * pi * epsilon_0 * (d/2)) = 0.

#### Question 20
A parallel plate capacitor is charged and then disconnected from the battery. If the plate separation is doubled, what happens to the energy stored in the capacitor?
*   (A) It is halved.
*   (B) It remains unchanged.
*   (C) It is doubled.
*   (D) It is quadrupled.
*   **Correct Answer:** (C)
*   **Explanation:** Since the battery is disconnected, the charge Q remains constant. The capacitance is C = epsilon_0 * A / d. Doubling the distance d halves the capacitance (C2 = C1 / 2). The energy stored is U = Q^2 / (2C). Since C is halved, U is doubled.

#### Question 21
What is the equivalent resistance between two opposite corners of a single wire loop forming a square of total resistance R?
*   (A) R
*   (B) R / 2
*   (C) R / 4
*   (D) R / 8
*   **Correct Answer:** (C)
*   **Explanation:** Opposite corners divide the square into two parallel pathways, each consisting of two sides of the square. Each pathway has a resistance of R/2. The equivalent parallel resistance is (R/2) / 2 = R/4.

#### Question 22
A proton enters a uniform magnetic field pointing along the positive z-axis. If the proton's velocity is directed along the positive x-axis, in which direction is the initial magnetic force?
*   (A) Positive y-axis
*   (B) Negative y-axis
*   (C) Positive z-axis
*   (D) Negative x-axis
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** The magnetic force is given by F = q * (v x B). The cross product of the positive x-axis (i) and the positive z-axis (k) is directed along the negative y-axis (-j).

#### Question 23
A long solenoid has n turns per unit length and carries a current I. What is the magnetic field inside the solenoid near its center?
*   (A) mu_0 * n * I / 2
*   (B) mu_0 * n * I
*   (C) 2 * mu_0 * n * I
*   (D) Zero
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** Applying Ampere's law, the magnetic field inside an ideal, infinitely long solenoid is constant and equal to mu_0 * n * I.

#### Question 24
Which Maxwell equation implies that magnetic monopoles do not exist?
*   (A) Gauss's Law for Electricity
*   (B) Gauss's Law for Magnetism
*   (C) Faraday's Law of Induction
*   (D) Ampere-Maxwell Law
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** Gauss's Law for Magnetism states that the net magnetic flux through any closed surface is zero (div B = 0), implying that magnetic field lines are closed loops and monopoles do not exist.

#### Question 25
A flat loop of area A and resistance R is rotated in a uniform magnetic field B. What is the maximum current induced in the loop if it completes f revolutions per second?
*   (A) 2 * pi * f * B * A / R
*   (B) f * B * A / R
*   (C) B * A / (R * f)
*   (D) Zero
*   **Correct Answer:** (A)
*   **Explanation:** The flux is phi = B * A * cos(omega * t). The induced emf is E = -d(phi)/dt = B * A * omega * sin(omega * t). The maximum emf is B * A * omega = 2 * pi * f * B * A. Dividing by R gives the maximum current.

#### Question 26
A capacitor C and an inductor L are connected in a closed loop. If the capacitor is initially charged, what is the frequency of the resulting electromagnetic oscillations?
*   (A) 1 / (2 * pi * L * C)
*   (B) 1 / (2 * pi * sqrt(L * C))
*   (C) sqrt(L * C) / (2 * pi)
*   (D) 2 * pi * sqrt(L * C)
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** The differential equation for an LC circuit is d^2(q)/dt^2 + q / (L*C) = 0. The angular frequency is omega = 1 / sqrt(L*C), which corresponds to a cyclic frequency of 1 / (2 * pi * sqrt(L * C)).

#### Question 27
What happens to the potential difference across a real battery if the current drawn from it is increased?
*   (A) It increases.
*   (B) It remains constant.
*   (C) It decreases.
*   (D) It drops to zero instantly.
*   **Correct Answer:** (C)
*   **Explanation:** The terminal voltage of a real battery is V = E - I * r, where E is the electromotive force and r is the internal resistance. Increasing the current I reduces the terminal voltage.

#### Question 28
What is the electric field at a distance r outside an infinitely long wire carrying a uniform linear charge density lambda?
*   (A) lambda / (2 * pi * epsilon_0 * r)
*   (B) lambda / (4 * pi * epsilon_0 * r^2)
*   (C) lambda * r / (2 * pi * epsilon_0)
*   (D) Zero
*   **Correct Answer:** (A)
*   **Explanation:** Applying Gauss's law using a cylindrical Gaussian surface of radius r and length L yields E * (2 * pi * r * L) = (lambda * L) / epsilon_0, which simplifies to E = lambda / (2 * pi * epsilon_0 * r).

#### Question 29
A dielectric slab is inserted into a parallel plate capacitor while it remains connected to a constant voltage battery. What happens to the capacitance and the charge?
*   (A) Capacitance increases, charge increases.
*   (B) Capacitance increases, charge decreases.
*   (C) Capacitance decreases, charge increases.
*   (D) Both remain unchanged.
*   **Correct Answer:** (A)
*   **Explanation:** Inserting a dielectric increases the capacitance by a factor of K (C = K * C0). Since the voltage V is held constant by the battery, the charge Q = C * V must also increase.

#### Question 30
Two parallel wires carry currents in opposite directions. How do the wires interact?
*   (A) They attract each other.
*   (B) They repel each other.
*   (C) They exert no force on each other.
*   (D) They rotate around each other.
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** Using the right-hand rule and the Lorentz force law, parallel wires carrying currents in opposite directions repel each other.

#### Question 31
What is the phase difference between the current and the voltage in a purely capacitive AC circuit?
*   (A) Current leads voltage by 90 degrees.
*   (B) Voltage leads current by 90 degrees.
*   (C) They are in phase.
*   (D) Current leads voltage by 180 degrees.
*   **Correct Answer:** (A)
*   **Explanation:** In a capacitor, current is proportional to the rate of change of voltage. Under sinusoidal conditions, the current leads the voltage by a phase angle of 90 degrees (pi/2 radians).

#### Question 32
A copper ring is dropped horizontally through a vertical magnetic field pointing upwards. What is the direction of the force acting on the ring as it enters the field?
*   (A) Downwards
*   (B) Upwards
*   (C) Horizontally to the left
*   (D) Zero
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** According to Lenz's law, the induced current in the ring opposes the change in magnetic flux. This results in an upward magnetic force that opposes the downward gravitational motion.

### Section 3: Thermodynamics (Questions 33 to 40)

#### Question 33
An ideal gas undergoes a free expansion into a vacuum within an insulated container. What are the changes in its temperature (dT) and entropy (dS)?
*   (A) dT = 0, dS = 0
*   (B) dT > 0, dS > 0
*   (C) dT = 0, dS > 0
*   (D) dT < 0, dS > 0
*   **Correct Answer:** (C)
*   **Explanation:** In a free expansion into a vacuum, no work is done (W = 0) and no heat is exchanged (Q = 0), meaning the internal energy remains constant (dU = 0). For an ideal gas, constant internal energy means the temperature does not change (dT = 0). However, the process is irreversible, so entropy increases (dS > 0).

#### Question 34
What is the average translational kinetic energy of a molecule in an ideal gas at temperature T?
*   (A) (1/2) * k * T
*   (B) (3/2) * k * T
*   (C) k * T
*   (D) (5/2) * k * T
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** According to the equipartition theorem, each translational degree of freedom contributes (1/2)*k*T to the energy of a molecule. Since there are three translational dimensions, the total average translational kinetic energy is (3/2)*k*T.

#### Question 35
If the pressure of an ideal gas is doubled while its volume is halved, what happens to its absolute temperature?
*   (A) It is doubled.
*   (B) It is halved.
*   (C) It remains unchanged.
*   (D) It is quadrupled.
*   **Correct Answer:** (C)
*   **Explanation:** From the ideal gas law, P * V = n * R * T. If P is replaced by 2P and V is replaced by V/2, the product P * V remains constant, meaning the temperature T remains unchanged.

#### Question 36
Under what conditions does a real gas behave most like an ideal gas?
*   (A) High pressure and low temperature
*   (B) Low pressure and high temperature
*   (C) Low pressure and low temperature
*   (D) High pressure and high temperature
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** At low pressure, the gas molecules are far apart, making their volume negligible. At high temperature, their kinetic energy is high enough to overcome intermolecular attractive forces.

#### Question 37
Which of the following processes is strictly reversible?
*   (A) Heat transfer across a finite temperature difference
*   (B) Frictionless quasi-static expansion of a gas
*   (C) Free expansion of an ideal gas
*   (D) Joule-Thomson expansion
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** A process is reversible only if it is quasi-static (infinitely slow) and occurs without dissipative forces such as friction.

#### Question 38
What is the change in entropy of a system during a cyclic, reversible engine process?
*   (A) Positive
*   (B) Negative
*   (C) Zero
*   (D) Infinite
*   **Correct Answer:** (C)
*   **Explanation:** Entropy is a state function. For any complete cycle that returns the system to its initial state, the net change in any state function must be zero.

#### Question 39
If a system absorbs 500 J of heat and does 200 J of work on its surroundings, what is the change in its internal energy?
*   (A) 700 J
*   (B) 300 J
*   (C) -300 J
*   (D) 200 J
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** According to the First Law of Thermodynamics, dU = Q - W. Substituting the values gives dU = 500 J - 200 J = 300 J.

#### Question 40
The work done in which of the following processes is equal to the negative change in internal energy?
*   (A) Isothermal
*   (B) Isobaric
*   (C) Adiabatic
*   (D) Isochoric
*   **Correct Answer:** (C)
*   **Explanation:** For an adiabatic process, Q = 0. The First Law of Thermodynamics yields dU = -W, meaning the work done by the system is equal to the decrease in its internal energy.

### Section 4: Waves, Optics & Modern Physics (Questions 41 to 50)

#### Question 41
A light wave propagates from water (n = 1.33) into air (n = 1.00). What is the critical angle for total internal reflection?
*   (A) arcsin(1.33)
*   (B) arcsin(0.75)
*   (C) arcsin(0.50)
*   (D) Total internal reflection cannot occur.
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** The critical angle is given by sin(theta_c) = n2 / n1. Here, n2 = 1.00 and n1 = 1.33, which yields sin(theta_c) = 1 / 1.33 = 3/4 = 0.75.

#### Question 42
In a single-slit diffraction experiment, the width of the slit is decreased. What happens to the width of the central maximum on the screen?
*   (A) It decreases.
*   (B) It remains unchanged.
*   (C) It increases.
*   (D) It oscillates.
*   **Correct Answer:** (C)
*   **Explanation:** The position of the first minimum is given by a * sin(theta) = lambda. Decreasing the slit width a increases the angle theta, widening the central maximum.

#### Question 43
What is the stopping potential in a photoelectric experiment if the maximum kinetic energy of the ejected electrons is 4.8 x 10^-19 Joules?
*   (A) 1.5 V
*   (B) 3.0 V
*   (C) 4.8 V
*   (D) 6.0 V
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** The stopping potential V_s is related to kinetic energy by K_max = e * V_s. Thus, V_s = K_max / e = (4.8 x 10^-19 J) / (1.6 x 10^-19 C) = 3.0 V.

#### Question 44
According to the Bohr model, what is the energy of the electron in the ground state of a hydrogen-like helium ion (He+, Z = 2)?
*   (A) -13.6 eV
*   (B) -27.2 eV
*   (C) -54.4 eV
*   (D) -3.4 eV
*   **Correct Answer:** (C)
*   **Explanation:** The energy levels of a hydrogen-like atom are E_n = -13.6 * Z^2 / n^2 eV. For He+ (Z = 2) in the ground state (n = 1), E1 = -13.6 * 4 / 1 = -54.4 eV.

#### Question 45
Two spaceships approach each other, each moving at a velocity of 0.6c relative to a stationary observer. What is their relative speed of approach according to the pilot of one spaceship?
*   (A) 1.20c
*   (B) 0.88c
*   (C) 0.96c
*   (D) 0.75c
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** Using the relativistic velocity addition formula: v_rel = (u + v) / (1 + u * v / c^2) = (0.6c + 0.6c) / (1 + 0.36) = 1.2c / 1.36 ≈ 0.88c.

#### Question 46
What is the rest mass of a photon?
*   (A) h * nu / c^2
*   (B) Zero
*   (C) 1.67 x 10^-27 kg
*   (D) Infinite
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** Photons always travel at the speed of light in a vacuum and possess zero rest mass. Their energy is entirely momentum-based.

#### Question 47
If a light source is moving away from an observer, how does the observed frequency compare to the source frequency?
*   (A) It is higher (blue-shifted).
*   (B) It is lower (red-shifted).
*   (C) It is identical.
*   (D) It drops to zero.
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** Due to the Doppler effect, when a light source recedes from an observer, the observed wavelength increases, resulting in a lower frequency (red-shift).

#### Question 48
Which of the following phenomena demonstrates the particle nature of light?
*   (A) Thin-film interference
*   (B) Double-slit diffraction
*   (C) Compton scattering
*   (D) Polarization
*   **Correct Answer:** (C)
*   **Explanation:** Compton scattering involves the collision of a photon and an electron, demonstrating momentum transfer that can only be explained by modeling light as localized particles.

#### Question 49
A radioactive sample has a half-life of 4 days. If the initial activity is A_0, what is the activity of the sample after 12 days?
*   (A) A_0 / 3
*   (B) A_0 / 8
*   (C) A_0 / 16
*   (D) A_0 / 64
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** The number of half-lives elapsed is n = 12 / 4 = 3. The remaining activity is A = A_0 * (1/2)^3 = A_0 / 8.

#### Question 50
Which quantum mechanical principle asserts that it is impossible to simultaneously measure the exact position and momentum of a particle?
*   (A) Pauli Exclusion Principle
*   (B) Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle
*   (C) De Broglie Hypothesis
*   (D) Schrodinger Wave Equation
*   **Correct Answer:** (B)
*   **Explanation:** The Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle states that the product of the uncertainties in position and momentum must be greater than or equal to h-bar / 2 (delta_x * delta_p >= h-bar/2).
